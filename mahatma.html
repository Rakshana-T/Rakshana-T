Early Life

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, was a prominent leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He studied law in England and later moved to South Africa, where he became involved in the civil rights struggle.

Independence Movement

Gandhi returned to India in 1915 and became a leader in the Indian National Congress. He advocated for nonviolent civil disobedience as a means to achieve independence from British rule. His campaigns and protests, such as the Salt March, played a crucial role in India's struggle for freedom.

Legacy

Gandhi's teachings of nonviolence, tolerance, and peaceful resistance continue to inspire people around the world. He is widely revered as the father of the nation in India and his birthday, October 2nd, is celebrated as the International Day of Non-Violence.
